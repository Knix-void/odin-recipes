Simple Hot Pockets

Now this one is kinda odd, because of a very simple reason. I am not actually sure what a Hot Pocket is supposed to be, and I refuse to find out. It's tastes good though, so, try it out.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of flour
  • salt, by your taste
  • ¾ cup of COLD butter (it's important that it's COLD)
  • 4 to 6 spoons of cold water

Preparing

  1. Add flour and butter and mix it until it's clumpy

  2. Add salt

  3. Slowly add water, and mix until clumpy but not sticky, if it's sticky you passed the point

  4. Let it chill for 1 hour

  5. Flour a surface and roll the dough out thin

  6. Cut into rectangles and fill it with filling (last time i made some, I fried some vegetable I had on the fridge, with a couple sliced ham)

  7. Oil the pan

  8. Lightly oil the top of the closed pockets

  9. Add some seasoning if have some

  10. Bake at 220°C for 20-25 minutes

Banter

The result should be crispy and really nice pastry, I think it's called that, I mean that should be the right english word. I made it a while ago, thinking about making some more this week, or the next.
